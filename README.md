# Global-Airbnb-Performance-Dashboard
Power BI Dashboard analyzing Airbnb performance across 10 global cities.
🏠 Global Airbnb Performance Dashboard
Built with Power BI | 10 Cities | 1,92,831 Listings

📌 Project Overview
This project is an end-to-end Power BI dashboard analyzing Airbnb's global performance across 10 major cities worldwide. The goal was to go beyond just displaying numbers — the dashboard tells a story about how Airbnb has grown, which cities lead in listings and reviews, how pricing varies across room types, and what trust signals hosts provide to guests.
The dashboard is divided into 3 interactive pages, each focused on a distinct area of analysis.

🗺️ Cities Analyzed
Mexico City · New York · Paris · Rome · Sydney · Rio de Janeiro · Cape Town · Istanbul · Bangkok · Hong Kong

📊 Dashboard Pages
Page 1 — New Listings
Tracks how Airbnb listings grew and declined from 2008 to 2021, broken down by room type (Entire Place, Hotel Room, Private Room, Shared Room). Key phases like Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline, Reinvention, and the COVID-19 impact are highlighted directly on the chart.
Page 2 — Overview & Pricing
Analyzes which cities have the most listings and reviews, the average pricing across room types, and overall city ratings. Includes an interactive navigation button to drill into detailed ratings.
Page 3 — Reviews & Trust
Examines customer review frequency patterns, seasonal review trends across cities, and host trust signals — including identity verification and profile picture status.

💡 Key Insights

Paris, NYC, and Sydney account for nearly 48% of total listings and reviews
Hotel rooms average $622/night — almost twice the price of private rooms ($322)
98.8% of customers wrote a review 3 times or less, showing mostly one-time reviewers
73.2% of Airbnb hosts are identity verified with a profile picture, building strong guest trust
Paris and Rome dominate review share from April to August, reflecting peak European summer travel
Mexico City and Rio de Janeiro are the best-rated cities overall; Hong Kong and Istanbul score the lowest on cleanliness and value
